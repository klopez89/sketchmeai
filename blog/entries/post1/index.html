Getting Started with SketchMeAi: Model Training and Generation Basics

Thursday, March 28th 2024

 

While you can start with generating images using the base SDXL model, this post is going to go over how you can generate images of yourself! To do this you will need to train an AI model using pictures of yourself.

 

Training your First Model

From the Models page, click Train New Model to open the New Custom Model form:

Your first name is a solid choice for your model name, but it can be anything as long as you don't use spaces or emojis ðŸ˜‹. In a future update, you'll be able to train the model on a style and any object. For this training, you'll train the base SDXL model on a person. Then upload a minimum of 10 images (max: 20). Important: The quality of images you provide as training data is the biggest driving factor to the quality of the trained model in regards to resemblance.

 

âœ… High quality training images look like:

  • Varied angles and distance
  • Well lit
  • High resolution (ideally 1024x1024+) Pictures taken from a modern smartphone will do.
  • Only the one person in each picture
  • Clear face images mixed with a few that include your upper torso

 

ðŸš« Low quality training images look like:

  • Same exact angle and distance
  • Poorly lit
  • Low resolution (below 1024x1024)
  • Only the one person in each picture
  • Multiple people in the shot
  • Blurry faces. Sunglasses. Big puffy coat and winter headgear.

 

Once your uploaded images have processed, click 'Start Training'. Congratulations, you've kicked off your very own AI model training, and when it's done it'll have a good idea about how you look.

 

Model training will take about 30 minutes for 10 images (and about 3 mins per image for anything more). Once training is finished, you will receive an email with a link to take you to the generation page with the newly trained model auto-selected.

Exploring the effects of Guidance Scale

Guidance scale (also known as classifier free guidance or cfg) controls how closely the generation should adhere to the prompt. A higher value enforces greater fidelity to the prompt, potentially leading to more accurate but less varied results, while a lower value allows for more creative interpretations.

 

The default value on SketchMeAi is 13. So let's fire off generations w/ the following guidance scale values to see how our latest image renders across the spectrum of values: 1,3,5,8,10,15,18,20.
