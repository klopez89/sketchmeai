Organize Your Generations with Collections

Tuesday, April 23rd 2024

 

As we've come to expect, the AI space evolves insanely fast! New AI tech papers seem to come every single day. Well one of those recent papers caught my attention for two reasons: a promise of higher-quality images and super easy integration into existing workflows. The paper was published on April 22nd 2024 by Nvidia's Toronto AI Lab titled, “Align Your Steps: Optimizing Sampling Schedules in Diffusion Models”. From here on out, when you see the acronym 'AYS', it is short for this new technique.

 

Think of diffusion models as architects, but they work slowly, like building a house brick by brick. This is due to their step-by-step plan, or 'blueprint'. A majority of recent diffusion tech announcements have been to improve the tools, not the blueprint. This new method, "Align Your Steps or AYS”, optimizes the blueprint, making building faster and the end result better.

 

Benefits of AYS:

  • Cheaper:In SketchMeAi, the default number of steps is 20 when generating an image, which costs on average $0.04 (when model is warm). With AYS, the technique is inherently based on 10 steps so that means generation cost drops a penny to $0.03/image.
  • Faster:At 20 denoising steps, an image takes about 18 seconds to generate. With AYS @ 10 steps, that number is cut in half to 9 seconds.
  • Super Easy Integration:This technique can be easily applied to existing workflows that use Stable Diffusion XL, which SketchMeAi does. It also works with SD1.5 and few other popular models.

 

Limitations of AYS:

  • Sampling Scheduler Specific:There are a number of sampling schedulers that one can use with diffusion models but from initial tests, the one that works the best w/ AYS (per the paper) is DPMSolverMultistepScheduler. Although we don't give you direct control on which scheduler to use on SketchMeAi, future techniques that require a different scheduler will mean AYS can't be used in combination.
  • Bizarre Outputs(sometimes): Just like in most AI workflows, not all generated output will be of high quality. This is still true with AYS. When the images are good, they can be really good. But when images are bad, they can be pretty bad. Almost comedic. I tend to see more bizarre outputs when using a person model (or LoRa). Since AYS is significantly cheaper and faster than 20 step generations, it more than makes up for bizarre output with the really bright gems.
